What This Site Covers

California's policy debates are usually framed in partisan terms that obscure the actual evidence. This site presents the data on contested questions — what wage laws actually produce, which redistricting maps favor whom, why California electricity costs roughly twice the national average, how the 2025 LA fires fit a pattern decades in the making — and lets the reader weigh competing claims with the receipts in front of them.

Each section is sourced from primary government data (Cal Fire, BLS QCEW, Cal-Access, the Legislative Analyst's Office), peer-reviewed studies, and industry filings. Where studies disagree, both findings are presented with their methodological strengths and weaknesses.
